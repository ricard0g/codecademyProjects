Your Instructors

We have a Priest, a Bible Scholar and the Pope himself!

Father Ignatius O'Malley

Devout Priest

Meet Father Ignatius — the man who blesses the bread before it graces your table. His eyes crinkle when he laughs, and his hands, rough from tilling the church garden, hold both rosary beads and secrets. When the moon hangs low, he slips into the confessional, whispering forgiveness to the shadows

Dr. Ezekiel Thorne

Esteemed Bible Scholar

Dr. Thorne — his office smells of old leather and curiosity. His desk, a battlefield of ink stains and half-finished theories. He wears tweed jackets with elbow patches, and his spectacles perch on the edge of his nose like curious birds. His students adore him. They call him “Doc,” and he chuckles, revealing a chipped tooth. His lectures? Riveting. He unravels the Bible like a detective—tracing metaphors, chasing lost parables

Pope Maximilian IX

Supreme Pontiff of Holy See

Pope Maximilian — the man who wears silk robes and carries the weight of centuries. His smile? A practiced curve, masking ancient doubts. When he blesses the crowds, his voice echoes through St. Peter’s Square, but his eyes search the skies. He loves the Sistine Chapel—the frescoes that tell stories of creation, sin, and redemption. Yet, when the tourists disperse, he gazes upward
